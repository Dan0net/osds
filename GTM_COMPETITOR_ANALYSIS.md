## Executive Summary

OSDS competes in a market with three distinct competitor categories — **high-commission marketplaces** (Rover at ~25%, Wag! at ~45%), **fixed-cost SaaS** (Time To Pet at $25–50/mo), and the **free DIY stack** (Venmo + Google Calendar + texting). The landscape has consolidated around Rover (taken private by Blackstone for $2.3B in 2024) while Wag! filed for bankruptcy in July 2025.

OSDS fills the gap between these extremes: **marketplace-quality tools at indie-friendly pricing** — 5% commission, no monthly fee, no owner fees, with a branded booking page that no other single tool provides.

The free DIY stack is OSDS's hardest competitor. Not Rover, not Time To Pet — it's **Venmo + Google Calendar + texting + Instagram**, which costs nothing beyond per-transaction fees. On pure price, OSDS loses to almost everything except Rover. **Price is not the GTM lever — time savings and professional appearance are.**

## Why 5% Exists — And Why It's Risky

The 5% gap exists because **OSDS is a fundamentally different business model**, not because competitors are overcharging. No one else in pet care charges below 15%.

### What Rover's 20% Pays For (That OSDS Doesn't Provide)

- $25K vet care guarantee (insurance underwriting)
- Background checks on every sitter
- 24/7 Trust & Safety team
- Customer support for disputes and emergencies
- Marketing/SEO that drives demand to sitters
- Payment fraud prevention
- Legal/compliance across 18 countries

### Risk Matrix

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Walkers don't value tools over DIY** | High | Booking page + payments must be dramatically better than Venmo + texting |
| **No support budget** | High | Self-service only. Extensive docs. No phone support |
| **Low perceived value at 5%** | Medium | Frame as "keep 95%" not "we're cheap." The savings *is* the value prop |
| **No acquisition budget** | Medium | Organic only: walker word-of-mouth, Facebook groups, Reddit, content |
| **Not competing with 20% — competing with 0%** | High | Must prove the free stack's hidden costs (time, lost bookings, unprofessional appearance) |

### Why It Could Work

- **Not a marketplace** — walkers bring their own clients. Closer to Shopify than Rover
- **Costs are genuinely lower** — no trust & safety, no insurance. Supabase + Netlify = near-zero infra
- **Stripe pass-through protects margin** — 5% + Stripe fees means OSDS always keeps the full 5%
- **Near the price floor** — below 5% is unsustainable. Hard for anyone to undercut

## The Free Stack: OSDS's Hardest Competitor

### What Walkers Actually Use ($0/month)

| Tool | Role | Limitations |
|---|---|---|
| Venmo / Zelle / Cash App | Payments | No invoicing, no recurring billing, no booking link |
| Google Calendar | Scheduling | No client-facing booking, no capacity management |
| Text / iMessage / WhatsApp | Communication | No booking workflow, no records, messages get buried |
| Instagram / Facebook / Nextdoor | Discovery & marketing | No booking, no payments, no transaction layer |
| Google Business Profile | Local SEO | No booking integration |

### What the Free Stack Lacks (OSDS's Opportunity)

| Gap | Severity | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **No self-service booking page** | Critical | Every booking requires a text conversation. Walker is the bottleneck for growth |
| **No booking ↔ payment link** | High | "Did Sarah pay for Tuesday?" requires checking Venmo and cross-referencing calendar |
| **No automated confirmations** | High | Forget one confirmation → angry client or no-show |
| **No cancellation/reschedule flow** | High | 4+ messages per reschedule |
| **No client/pet records** | Medium | Allergies, vet info, gate codes scattered in text threads |
| **No professional presence** | Medium | Looks like a side hustle. Hard to raise prices |
| **No recurring booking automation** | Medium | Weekly clients must be manually managed |
| **No capacity management** | Medium | Double-bookings resolved by texting |

### The Hidden Time Cost of "Free"

The free stack costs walkers an estimated **~13 hours/month** in admin:

| Task | Monthly Total |
|---|---|
| Booking via text (back-and-forth) | 6.7 hours |
| Calendar management | 2.7 hours |
| Rescheduling conversations | 1.3 hours |
| Confirmation texts | 1.3 hours |
| Payment tracking | 1.0 hours |
| **Total** | **~13 hours/month** |

At $30/walk, 13 hours ≈ 26 walks = **$780/month in opportunity cost** — far more than OSDS's $214 fee. But walkers don't feel this cost because it happens between walks, in the evenings, on their phone.

## GTM Strategy

### The Core Insight

**Price is not the lever.** At 80 walks/month, OSDS costs more than every option except Rover. The lever is **time savings** (~13 hrs/month of admin eliminated) and **professional appearance** (branded booking page vs "text me").

### 1. Target: The "Drowning in Texts" Walker

- Not the 5-client hobbyist (happy with Venmo)
- Not the 50-client business owner (already on Time To Pet)
- The **15–30 client walker** who's outgrown texting but hasn't committed to SaaS
- Losing bookings because clients can't self-schedule
- Spending evenings doing admin instead of earning

### 2. Channels: Go Where Walkers Already Are

- **Facebook Groups**: "Dog Walkers of [City]", "Pet Sitters Unite" — tight-knit communities where walkers share tools and frustrations
- **Instagram DMs**: Target walkers with 500–5,000 followers who post walk photos but have no booking link in bio
- **Reddit**: r/dogwalking, r/petsitting, r/smallbusiness
- **Pet care associations**: NAPPS, PSI — Time To Pet's proven playbook
- **Nextdoor**: Walkers recommending walkers

### 3. Hook: "Your Own Booking Page in 5 Minutes"

- The activation moment is seeing `yourname.onestopdog.shop` live with their services, prices, and availability
- Zero setup friction — no credit card, no configuration wizard
- Share the link on Instagram bio, Facebook, business card → bookings start flowing

### 4. Wedge Strategy: Replace One Tool First

- Don't try to replace the whole stack at once
- Start as the **booking page + payment collection** — replaces "text me to book"
- Once on OSDS for bookings, expand to scheduling, client management, pet profiles
- **iCal sync is the bridge** — walkers keep Google Calendar while adding booking/payment on top

### 5. Prove the Math

- "You spent 13 hours last month confirming bookings by text. That's 4 walks you didn't do."
- "At $30/walk, OSDS costs you $1.50 per walk. Your time is worth more."
- "Clients who can self-book rebook 40% more often" (validate with early users)

### 6. Competitive Positioning

| Against... | OSDS Message |
|---|---|
| **Free Stack** | "The free stack costs 13 hours/month of admin. OSDS costs $1.50/walk and gives you your evenings back." |
| **Rover** | "Keep 95% of what you earn instead of 75%. Own your brand. Own your clients." |
| **Time To Pet** | "No public booking page — clients can't find and book you. No monthly fee — pay nothing until you earn." |
| **Square/Wix** | "Built for dog walkers, not restaurants. Pet profiles, walk scheduling, hold-based bookings — all purpose-built." |

### 7. The Trade-Off (Be Honest About It)

OSDS does **not** provide demand. Rover's value is its marketplace liquidity — 41M+ bookings means pet owners come to the platform looking for sitters. OSDS walkers must bring their own clients. The 5% vs 25% commission gap is the reward for doing your own customer acquisition.

**Target customer:** Established walkers who already have clients and are tired of giving Rover 20%, or growing walkers who've outgrown the free stack. Not brand-new walkers looking for their first booking.

## Who Will Switch to OSDS — And Who Won't

### Will Switch

| Persona | Pain Point | OSDS Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **20+ client walker** | Free stack breaks down at scale. Text booking unmanageable | Self-service booking page, automated confirmations |
| **"Raising prices" walker** | Charging $40–50/walk, clients expect professional booking | Branded page adds perceived value that justifies rates |
| **Coverage-needer** | Transferring clients to backup walker = forwarding texts | Backup walker sees full schedule + client info |
| **Growth-oriented walker** | Wants to hire help, add services, scale | Platform grows with them |
| **Rover refugee** | Tired of giving 20% on clients they sourced | 5% commission, walker owns the relationship |

### Won't Switch

| Persona | Why |
|---|---|
| **5-client walker** | No pain points. Texting works fine. Doesn't need software |
| **Cash-only walker** | Digital records are a disadvantage |
| **Tech-averse walker** | Texting is all they know |
| **50+ client business** | Already invested in Time To Pet. Switching cost too high |

## Moat Analysis

### What Is NOT a Moat

- **5% commission** — any competitor can match this. Price is not a moat
- **Features** — Time To Pet can add a booking page. Wix can add pet fields
- **Technology** — React + Supabase + Netlify is commodity infrastructure

### Moats to Build

| Moat | How | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **URL/brand equity** | `walkername.onestopdog.shop` on business cards, Instagram bios, shared by clients | 3–6 months |
| **Switching costs** | 50+ clients booking through OSDS = painful to migrate | 6–12 months |
| **Data gravity** | Years of pet records, booking history, client preferences | 12+ months |
| **Community** | Walker-to-walker referrals, cross-referral features | 12+ months |

### The Real Threat: Time To Pet

Time To Pet is purpose-built for pet care, has 4,000 businesses, and is bootstrapped (sustainable). If they add a public booking page, they cover OSDS's biggest differentiator. **The booking page is the wedge — move fast.**

## Key Takeaways

1. **OSDS's real competitor is inertia** — walkers who think "texting works fine." GTM must overcome this with a visceral demo of what they're missing.

2. **Price only wins below ~$600/month revenue.** Above that, fixed-cost SaaS is cheaper. The value prop at higher volumes must be features + convenience.

3. **The booking page is the killer feature.** No other tool gives independent walkers a branded, public, self-service booking page with integrated payments and pet profiles. This is the activation moment and the GTM hook.

4. **Compete on time, not cost.** Lead with "stop spending 13 hours/month on admin" — not "we're cheaper than Rover."

5. **The commission model is double-edged.** Zero barrier to start, but high-volume walkers pay more than SaaS. Consider a hybrid model long-term.

6. **The honest framing:** "The free stack works. But it only works when *you* work. You are the booking system, the payment tracker, the confirmation sender. OSDS automates the parts of running a business that aren't walking dogs."
