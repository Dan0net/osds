# OSDS Competitor Analysis

> Last updated: March 2026

## Executive Summary

OSDS competes in the pet care services market — but with a fundamentally different model. Where marketplaces like Rover take 25%+ of every booking, OSDS charges walkers/sitters a **flat 5% commission** and gives them their own branded booking page. The competitive landscape has consolidated around Rover (taken private by Blackstone for $2.3B) while Wag! filed for bankruptcy in July 2025. Regional players hold niches, but no major new entrant has emerged since 2022.

## OSDS Positioning

### The Market Gap

The pet care platform market has consolidated into two extremes:
1. **High-commission marketplaces** (Rover at ~25%, Wag! at ~45%) that provide demand but take a large cut
2. **B2B SaaS tools** (Time To Pet at $25-50/mo) that give independence but no customer acquisition

OSDS fills the gap: **marketplace-quality tools at indie-friendly pricing.**

### OSDS Competitive Advantages

| Advantage | vs Marketplaces (Rover, Wag!) | vs SaaS (Time To Pet) |
|---|---|---|
| **5% commission** | Walkers keep 95% vs 75-55% | No upfront monthly cost |
| **Walker-branded pages** | Walker builds their own brand, not Rover's | Similar (white-label) |
| **No owner fees** | Owners pay nothing extra | N/A |
| **Booking + payments** | Same convenience as marketplace | Comparable |
| **No lock-in** | Walker owns the relationship | Similar |

### Key Differentiators from Rover

| | Rover | OSDS |
|---|---|---|
| Who the customer "belongs to" | Rover | The walker |
| Walker commission | ~20% | **5%** |
| Owner fees | 5-7% | **$0** |
| Walker branding | Rover-branded profile | Walker's own branded page |
| Discovery/SEO | Rover provides demand | Walker does own marketing |
| Insurance/guarantee | Rover Guarantee ($25K) | Walker arranges own insurance |
| Background checks | Rover-managed | Walker provides own |

### The Trade-Off (Be Honest About It)

OSDS does **not** provide demand. Rover's value is its marketplace liquidity — 41M+ bookings means pet owners come to the platform looking for sitters. OSDS walkers need to bring their own clients (existing base, word of mouth, local marketing, social media). The 5% vs 25% commission gap is the reward for doing your own customer acquisition.

**Target customer:** Established walkers/sitters who already have clients and are tired of giving Rover 20%. Not brand-new walkers looking for their first booking.
